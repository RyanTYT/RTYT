Yes, this is the THIRD iteration of a complete revamp of my personal website. kept v2 commits and history in my repo (and I believe v1 as well but I'm not so sure). 

## v1 and v2

The issue I had with v1 was that it was fully designed by me and I'm not ashamed to say the design was REALLY atrocious. You couldn't even force me to look back on that design. Sure there were some nice components like the landing page was a full picture of me, that you could scroll and it would have an effect of snapping away; there was also an infinitely scrollable and automatically horizontally scrolling component showcasing my modules and grades and a timeline branch fully made (almost from scratch) that animated really well while scrolling. BUT the colour scheme was horrendous and the designs of different components just did NOT integrate or meld well together. It felt like pulling together different coloured gems, smushing them together and dumping it in a pile of sh** (*pardon my french*).

Anyway, v2 had a design that was better but much more plain - full white, clean design. Mostly because it was designed by Claude quickly. However, there were many design details for that website I missed and didn't really fix up - because it was made by claude early on, the code wasn't very clean and there were a lot of moving parts that were too hard to follow easily. Furthermore, it felt too "un-me". There was no character to the site, let alone cool components that I spent time building for fun. It felt way too corporate for a personal website.

## v3

I know I basically sh** on Claude in the previous paragraph but full disclosure, v3 was basically almost completely made with Claude and a ton of prompting over around 3 days. As they say, "a tool is only as good as the person who wields it", and I wasn't very good at using Claude, I'll admit - especially since I didn't want to pay for extended usage. However, having interfaced with LLM tools so much more in Binance, I now have a better grasp on when I'm going down the wrong route and changing directions early. In particular, a long prompt doesn't always mean a good prompt. LLMs seem to like to have a specific strength or skill so for this version, I had it focus on producing design mockups for me to look at and choose from first before anything. With this, I was able to meld together some design choices and gain further inspiration from the Claude.

Overall, this v3 website is an amalgamation of about 3 different designs proposed by Claude, as well as further design enhancement proposals by me. The terminal view was one of the proposals by AI, for which I realised an immersive experience would be interesting - after all, I use Neovim which I think is quite unique. So, the colour palette and design closely follows my IDE experience with Neovim and how I use it, with some common shortcuts inbuilt in the "IDE". Following this, the landing page design closely follows the colour palette and font and remain simple enough to look through. The full timeline view is also a fun component I always liked seeing on other sites (i.e. animations while scrolling).

To top it all off, I added a "chatbot" functionality for you to try! and you can also leave me some feedback or a review or just say hi in a public Google Form I opened attached to my website! Anyway, I'm glad you ended here so please enjoy!! - I finally feel somewhat proud and satisfied with the design of my website; i can see this design persisting for quite a while, YAY!!
